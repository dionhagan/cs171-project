Our team’s major goal for this project was to visualize a college acceptance prediction algorithm in order to provide high schools students applying to colleges with an easy way to gather information about their probability of acceptance and therefore allow them to make more informed decisions about which schools to apply to. We used a random forest algorithm created from data on students who had applied to the top 25 US colleges in order to create the predictions. We then visualized this data as well as the predictions through two major visualizations, the interactive visualization and the data drill-down visualization.

With the interactive visualization, which is shown on the first page of the site, we wanted students and their parents to be able to see their own chances of acceptance at different schools. Our objectives were to both allow students to see both their probabilities of getting into different schools and also what they could do in order to best increase their chances at various schools. For this reason, we created a graph where students enter all of their information and see their percentages of acceptance either to all Ivies,

all non-Ivies, or all of the top 25 colleges. We then added a “save scenario” feature, where students could enter multiple combinations of GPAs, test scores, etc., and view each of them simultaneously as different colored points on the graph in order to compare these different scenarios and decide where to best focus their time. We also included a 3-dimensional visualization which gave users a different perspective on which schools they are most likely to be accepted into based on physical space. The schools that are easier for the student to get into are closer in space while the reach schools are physically farther away, giving users a quick and very easily interpreted image of which schools are their best bet.

The data drill-down, which is shown on the second page of the site, allows users to see more general trends in order to understand which factors are seen as more important by certain schools in terms of admission decisions. Upon opening the page, users see a bar graph showing the overall importance of each main factor in terms of admission. Users can then dig deeper by clicking on one of the specific schools and choosing

which schools they would like to examine, and are then provided with a series of visualizations showing the percentage importance of certain factors at each of those schools. We thought that this would be the best way to allow students and parents to see the bigger, overall picture as well as the personalized information applicable to the factors and schools in which they are most interested. We also created a heat map showing which factors increase or decrease chance of acceptance at each of the chosen schools.

Throughout this process, we learned a lot about challenges in terms of best visualizing a large amount of information. We found that there was a delicate balance between showing the most amount of information possible and creating an easily interpreted visualization, and evolved and changed many ideas in order to best achieve this balance. We believe that the visualizations we ended up creating were an effective way to provide high school students and their parents with the information that they need in order to make the best decisions about applying to colleges.
